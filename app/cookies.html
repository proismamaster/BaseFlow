Cookie & Local Storage Policy

Last updated: July 2026

BaseFlow does not use cookies for tracking, advertising, or analytics. As a fully client-side app with no backend, there is no server to set them and nothing to track. It does, however, use two browser technologies to make the app work offline and remember your work — this page explains both.

localStorage

Used to save data locally in your browser, so it's there next time you open the app. Nothing here is ever sent to a server.

What's storedWhy
Your flowchart (nodes, arrows, variables)So your work isn't lost when you close the tab
Selected language & theme (including custom colors)So your preferences persist between visits

Service Worker cache

BaseFlow registers a Service Worker that caches the app's own files (HTML, CSS, JS, icons) so it keeps working without an internet connection, like a native app. This cache only ever contains the application's own code — never your personal data.

Third-party cookies

None are set by BaseFlow. When the footer loads contributor avatars from GitHub, or the tutorial loads the Shepherd.js library from jsDelivr, those third parties may handle the request per their own cookie/privacy policies — see the Privacy Policy for details. BaseFlow does not embed any advertising or analytics cookies of its own.

Managing or clearing this data

You can clear everything BaseFlow has stored at any time from your browser settings (clear site data / clear browsing data for this site), or by clearing your browser's cache and site storage. Doing so will remove your saved flowchart and preferences and reset the app to its defaults.
