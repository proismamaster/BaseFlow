Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

BaseFlow is a free, open-source, entirely client-side application: it runs in your browser and has no backend server, no user accounts, and no login. This page explains, in plain terms, what that means for your data.

Data we collect

None. BaseFlow does not have a server to send your data to, so it cannot collect, store, or transmit your flowcharts, variables, console input, or any personal information. Everything you create stays on your own device.

What is stored on your device

  • Your flowchart and settings (nodes, variables, language, theme, custom theme colors) are saved locally in your browser's localStorage, only so the app can restore your work when you come back. This data never leaves your device.
  • Offline files (HTML/CSS/JS) are cached by a Service Worker so the app keeps working without an internet connection. See the Cookie & Local Storage Policy for details.

Third-party requests

A couple of features load resources from third-party providers, only when you use them:

  • jsDelivr CDN serves the Shepherd.js library used for the in-app guided tutorials.
  • GitHub's API and image CDN are used to show the project's contributor avatars in the footer, and to link to the source code.

These providers may see the request (e.g. your IP address and browser user agent) according to their own privacy policies, exactly as any website loading an external resource would. BaseFlow itself does not add any tracking, analytics, or advertising script.

Children's privacy

Since BaseFlow collects no personal data whatsoever from anyone, it can be used safely by learners of any age, including in classrooms.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes (for example if a future version adds an optional online feature), this page will be updated and the date above will reflect it.
